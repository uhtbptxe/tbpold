District Conference Frequently Asked Questions

Who: Any member and/or officer of a District 10 Chapter of Tau Beta Pi (This includes prospective members who will, most likely, be officers in the following semester.)
When: 8:30 a.m., Saturday, February 7, 2004
Where: University of Texas at Austin (TX Alpha chapter) -- Austin, TX
How: By car (carpool, vanpool, whatever)
Why: Officer training, Engineering Futures Session, ICE (Interactive Chapter Exchange), good food, good friends, ...
How much?: We will pay for Friday and Saturday nights' motel stay (4 students per room), lunch and dinner on Saturday, and car travel expenses for up to four people. "Travel expenses" means that we'll cover travel for at least 4 people from your chapter at the rate of $0.25 per mile and $0.05 per passenger per mile.  Save your receipts!
Needs: Headcount!!!! We need a list of the names of the people from your chapter who will be attending this semester's district conference. This may sound foolish, but please also include the gender of each of these people, as I will have to construct a room list, and I need to assign rooms based on this vital information! The last ifnromation to include is whether any attendees have dietary restrictions or special needs that we can prepare for before-hand. I need this list by January 31st, so please start working on it ASAP.

Other Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Are airline tickets reimbursed for travel to the district conference?
A: I wish, but no. Despite the size of our district, reimbursement of airline tickets is much too costly. As you may have read above, we will make it a priority to reimburse travel for 4 attendees in one car from each chapter. After that, we will reimburse what we can for chapters who brought more than 4 members.  Please contact us if you plan to bring more than 1 car load.

Q: Can I bring a guest?
A: Yes, but Tau Beta Pi cannot pay for travel, meals, or accommodations for that person / those people.  If you (or someone from your chapter) want to bring a guest, it's best they contact the conference organizer to work out a plan.  They can almost definitely stay in the same hotel and eat with the group, but it won't be covered.

Q: Can a prospective member attend?
A: Yes.  As mentioned above, promising members (i.e. candidates for officer positions) are encouraged to attend in order to take advantage of chapter operations training and the trip to Austin.

Q: What else do I need to know?
A: This should be enough to allow you to start getting names together fo make the trip to Austin February 6th or 7th.  In the next 2 weeks, the district directors will send out more information including directions to the hotel, the agenda and chapter operations material, directions to UT Austin, and information regarding expenses.
